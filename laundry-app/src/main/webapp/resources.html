Stain Removal A-Z

Blood

1. For a fresh blood stain, soak in cold salty water, following with a vinegar wash, and then rinse.
2. For dried blood stains, mix borax and water and apply it to the stain. Let it dry, then brush it off.


Chocolate

Blot the stain with cold water, then blot with mild laundry detergent. Rinse thoroughly. If the stain persists, blot with detergent and a few drops of ammonia and water. Then rinse. If that doesn’t work, try blotting wit an enzyme-based detergent.


Coffee

Blot the stain with cool water and rinse. Then mix laundry detergent and water and blot, then rinse. If that does not work, repeat that step, but with a little white vinegar added, and rinse. As a final step, add some hydrogen peroxide.


Deodorant

Mix a spot stain remover and lukewarm water. Place the stain face-down on cloth and sponge the back of it with the mixture. Let it stand several minutes then rinse. Repeat until the stain is mostly removed and rub liquid enzyme detergent into it. Wash it in hot water, and if fabric discolor, blot with white vinegar to try to restore it. Then rinse.


Grass

Brush white non-gel toothpaste into the stain using an soft toothbrush, rinse, and wash.


Grease

Pour boiling water through the grease spot, then dust with bicarb soda until it’s worked into the stain. Then wash normal.


Hair Dye

Clothing or fabrics stained with hair dye should be washed in warm water, with white vinegar and detergent. Do this in a sink or container, adding about 2 tablespoons of detergent and 2 cups of white vinegar to a gallon of warm water. Let it soak for several hours.


Ink

The stain should be placed face-down on a white paper towel and sponge with rubbing alcohol or rub laundry detergent into stain. Repeat, rinse, launder.


Permanent Marker Stains

Pour water through the stain before it dries, until pigments are removed. Allow to dry, then sponge with dry-cleaning solvent. Allow to dry, then rub liquid household cleaner into the stain and rinse. Soak stain in warm water to which 1 to 4 tablespoons of household ammonia have been added for several hours, then rinse and repeat if necessary and launder.


Ketchup

Use a dull knife or spoon to remove as much ketchup as possible. Then flush the back of the stain with cold water and rub a liquid enzyme detergent into the stain and let stand several minutes. Then rub the fabric between your thumbs while soaking. Then rinse. Then sponge with white vinegar. Repeat until most is removed. Then apply spot stain remover and let stand for several minutes. Launder with an enzyme detergent. If the stain remains, rub an enzyme detergent into the stain and then soak garment in warm water for up to 30 minutes or until the stain is removed. Rinse well.


Lipstick

Remove lipstick stains by rubbing vegetable oil into the stain and let it sit on the spot for 15 to 30 minutes. Then sponge the area with a little ammonia. Work in undiluted liquid dish soap. Then launder.


Mildew

Soak overnight in lemon juice or salty water, then wash in warm soapy water, rinse well and dry in the sun.


Mustard

Treat with a prewash stain remover or dampen with water and rub with bar soap. Launder with an all-fabric bleach.


Nail Polish

Apply nail polish remover to the back of the stain while laying the fabric on white absorbent towels, replacing the towels when needed. Then rinse and launder.


Urine Pet Stain

Blot the mark with a dry sponge to absorb as much liquid as possible, then dab with undiluted vinegar. Then sponge with cold water. Then launder.


Red Wine

Blot with paper towels or a dry cloth, then sprinkle with white wine to neutralize the stain, or water, and blot again. Remove the remaining stain with a clean cloth and methylated spirits.


Sweat

Soak garment for an hour in enough water to cover and 1/4 to 1/2 cup of salt. Wash as normal and dry in the sunshine.

The Five Essentials of Basic Laundry

Select the Right Water Temperature

a. Cold water works well for delicate fabrics.
b. Cold water works well for items that might shrink.
c. Cold water works well for sensitive dark colors that tend to bleed.
d. Cold water also saves you money by reducing the cost of utilities.
e. Warm water is appropriate for moderately soiled clothing.
f. Warm water is appropriate for human-made fabrics.
g. Hot water should be used for bedding and towels.
h. Hot water should be used for cotton whites.
i. Hot water should be used for heavily stained work clothes, specifically those with oil or grease stains.


Select the Right Detergent

a. Powdered detergents work well for general loads and are usually less expensive.
b. Liquid detergents are good for pretreating stains.
c. Single dose pods, packs, and tablets are convenient and easy to use, more tend to be more expensive.


Remove Stains as Soon as Possible

a. Rinse or soak the garment in cold water.
b. Apply a stain remover.
c. Put it in the washer and wash it.
d. Repeat if the stain is still there. Do not put in the dryer until the stain is removed.


Sort into Like Colors

a. Keep light with light, dark with dark, white with white.
b. Bleach goes with whites, not with anything else.


To Reduce Wrinkles

a. Use a fabric softener or dryer sheet.
b. Select the lowest dryer temperature setting possible.
c. Remove clothing from the dryer as soon as they are dry and immediately fold or hang them.
